# Guide: Disputing Fraudulent Transactions

This guide outlines the standard operating procedure for customers who need to dispute a transaction they believe to be fraudulent or unauthorized on their dBank account.

## Immediate Actions
If a customer suspects fraud, time is of the essence.
1. **Lock the Card:** Customers should immediately lock their debit or credit card using the dBank Mobile App or Web Portal to prevent further unauthorized charges.
2. **Contact Fraud Support:** Call the 24/7 dBank Fraud Hotline at 1-800-555-0199.

## The Dispute Process

### Step 1: Identify the Transaction
The customer must review their statement and clearly identify the transaction(s) in question. They need to provide:
- Date of the transaction.
- Merchant name.
- Transaction amount.

### Step 2: File the Dispute
Disputes can be filed in three ways:
1. **Online/Mobile App:** Navigate to the specific transaction, click "Report an Issue," and select "I don't recognize this transaction." This is the fastest method.
2. **Phone:** Call the Fraud Hotline. A representative will file the dispute on the customer's behalf.
3. **In-Branch:** Visit a local dBank branch to file paperwork with a teller.

### Step 3: Investigation
Once a dispute is filed, dBank initiates an investigation.
- **Provisional Credit:** By law, dBank may provide a provisional credit to the customer's account for the disputed amount within 10 business days while the investigation is ongoing.
- **Merchant Contact:** dBank will contact the merchant's acquiring bank to request documentation (e.g., receipts, IP logs, delivery confirmation) proving the charge was authorized.
- **Timeline:** The investigation can take up to 45 days (or 90 days for international transactions or new accounts).

### Step 4: Resolution
- **Favorable Outcome:** If the investigation proves fraud, the provisional credit becomes permanent, and the customer is not liable for the charge.
- **Unfavorable Outcome:** If the merchant provides compelling evidence that the charge was authorized (e.g., goods were delivered to the customer's address, or a chip-and-PIN transaction occurred), the dispute will be denied. The provisional credit will be reversed, and the customer will be notified in writing with an explanation.

## Zero Liability Protection
dBank offers Zero Liability Protection on all its consumer credit and debit cards. This means customers are not held responsible for unauthorized transactions if they report them promptly.
